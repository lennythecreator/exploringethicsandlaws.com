Introduction

Over the last decade, artificial intelligence has undergone transformative advancements, changing the way we perceive and interact with technology. While the roots of artificial intelligence were sown in the early 1900s, in the 1950s was where significant strides were taken. However, over the last 10 years, we have witnessed an unprecedented rise in the development of AI from machine learning algorithms enabling personalized recommendations to the proliferation of natural language processing facilitating human-computer interaction. This journey from conceptual foundations to tangible progress, underscores the trajectory of AI, setting the stage for further exploration and refinement in the years to come. Siri, the virtual assistant that has become an integral part of Apple's ecosystem, emerged from the collaborative efforts of a startup company and its innovative founders. Siri Inc. was established in 2007 by Dag Kittlaus, Adam Cheyer, and Tom Gruber, envisioning a revolutionary voice-controlled interface. Apple recognized the potential of Siri and its advanced capabilities, leading to its acquisition by the tech giant in 2010. Subsequently, Siri made its debut on the iPhone 4S in 2011, marking a significant leap in the realm of voice-activated personal assistants and paving the way for the integration of similar technologies across various devices. Apple's Siri faced privacy concerns that gained prominence around 2019 when reports emerged about the inadvertent collection of user audio recordings. It was revealed that contractors hired by Apple to enhance Siri's performance had access to snippets of users' conversations. This sparked a debate about the balance between improving voice recognition technology and safeguarding user privacy. In response to the outcry, Apple took swift measures to address the issue. They temporarily suspended the grading program, allowing users to opt-in or opt-out of audio sample sharing, and introduced more transparent privacy policies. These changes aimed to assure users that Apple was committed to protecting their privacy while continuing to refine Siri's capabilities. The incident highlighted the evolving challenges tech companies face in balancing innovation with user privacy concerns.
